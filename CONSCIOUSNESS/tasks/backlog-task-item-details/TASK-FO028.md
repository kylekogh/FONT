# TASK-FO028: Design meta-currency system — earned per run, progression tracks

## What

Design the outside-game loop before building it. What is earned, how much, what it unlocks, and what it's called in FONT vocabulary.

## The currency

Name candidates — choose the one that fits the demo's first faction:
- **Ardour** — the Kindled's standing track. Already canon. Using it as meta-currency means "your runs accumulate standing even when you lose." Strong FONT grounding.
- **Depth** — how far you descended. Universal to all factions; ties to the dungeon's fundamental pull.
- **Ember** — something left burning after a run, whatever happened. Poetic, faction-neutral.

**Recommended: Ardour for the Kindled-first demo.** Each run earns Ardour. Losing earns less; winning earns more; going deeper before dying earns more than dying shallow. In FONT terms: even a failed run is a step on the path.

## The progression tracks (at least 3)

1. **The Path** — unlock new starting options (a different starting relic, a bonus starting card). Costs escalate. This is the primary power progression.
2. **The Record** — unlock lore fragments (descended traces from the founding myth, misread by scholars but glimpsable by a long-running player). Costs are flat; unlocks are narrative, not mechanical.
3. **The Roster** — unlock access to additional classes or factions (future DIRECT-FO002 content). Gates the content expansion without blocking early play.

## Done when

- Currency name and earning formula are documented
- All 3 progression tracks have defined unlock lists and costs
- The earning formula is balanced (a first run should earn something; a perfect run should feel meaningfully more rewarding)
- Kyle has confirmed the tracks feel right in FONT terms

## Notes

The Record track (lore fragments) is the one that rewards the most dedicated players with the thing they actually want — glimpses of what the dungeon really is. A player who has earned 50 Ardour across many runs and finally sees a fragment that hints at old Sora is getting something no amount of money can buy. Design the fragments in FONT's founding myth language — worn, partial, misreadable.
